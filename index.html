Water

The Moo Duk Kwan Academy is very unique because it has only one founder and one grandmaster. It is a very traditional art, following values. "Ru Pa" means "water flows down always branching off but staying the same."

Fire

The fire we have in our art is the constant bombardment of struggle in our lives and the energy that we use.

Balance

When the two forces meet -water and fire- they create balance. Which is shown in the Korean flag with "um" and "yang". Soo Bahk Do Moo Duk Kwan was created to bring us back to tradition!

Jo Kyo Ian Constable

Dan Bon: 32609. A student of Sa Bom Nim Joseph Poppo, Ian began his Soo Bahk Do training at age 4 at Hong's Karate in Liberty, NY. With nearly 30 years of experience under his belt, Ian has competed in many tournaments and had won many grand championships. Kyo Sa Nim Patrick O'Leary

I started training under Joe Poppo at the Moo Duk Kwan Academy in Liberty NY in August of 1989. I received my Cho Dan (Black belt) in April of 1993 after training for just over three years. I received my Jo Kyo certification (Assistant instructor) in February of 2012 and in July of 2012 achieved the rank of E Dan (2nd degree Black belt). I continued to pursue the next level of training achieving my Sam Dan (3rd degree Black belt) in April of 2015. I received my Kyo Sa (Instructor) certification shortly after in April of 2015.

Sa Bom Nim Joseph Poppo

Sa Bom Nim Joseph Poppo began his Moo Duk Kwan training in September of 1984 in Monticello, New York. He started teaching under his instructor in the summer 1988 in Liberty, New York. Sa Bom Nim Poppo received Cho Dan in the fall of 1988, Kyo Sa in the fall 1989 and his E Dan in the fall of 1990. Sa Bom Nim Poppo opened the Moo Duk Kwan Academy in August of 1991. He received Sam Dan in the fall of 1993; and Sa Bom as well as Sa Dan in the fall of 1998. His instructor is Sa Bom Nim Jere Klien. Sa Bom Nim Poppo's goal for the Moo Duk Kwan Academy was for the school to transcend his life span. The family of Dans in the Moo Duk Kwan Academy has grown strong thanks to the efforts of Sa Bom Nim Poppo. Another goal of Sa Bom Nim Poppo's is that he wants the Moo Duk Kwan Academy to be a safe house for traditional Moo Duk Kwan training. Written by Kevin Horowitz (June 2005)
